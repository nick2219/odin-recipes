Quiche

Description

Quiche (/ˈkiːʃ/ KEESH) is a French tart consisting of pastry crust filled with savoury custard and pieces of cheese, meat, seafood or vegetables. The best-known variant is quiche Lorraine, which includes lardons or bacon. Quiche can be served hot or cold. It is popular worldwide.


  • 1(9 inch) deep-dish pie crust
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup sliced onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped zucchini
  • 1 large tomato,sliced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Colby-Jack cheese

Directions

  1. Step1

    2. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).

  2. Step 2

    3. Bake pie crust in preheated oven until firm, about 8 minutes. Remove crust from the oven and set aside. Reduce oven heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).

  3. Step 3

    4. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook and stir onion, bell pepper, mushrooms, and zucchini in the hot oil until soft, 5 to 7 minutes. Remove vegetables from skillet and set aside.

  4. Step 4

    5. Sprinkle tomato slices with flour and basil; cook in the skillet over medium heat for 1 minute per side.

  5. Step 5

    6. Whisk milk, eggs, salt, and pepper together in a small bowl.

  6. Step 6

    7. Spread 1 cup Colby-Jack cheese in the bottom of pie crust. Layer vegetable mixture over the cheese, then top with tomato slices. Pour egg mixture over top and sprinkle with remaining Colby-Jack.

  7. Step 7

    8. Bake in the oven until a knife inserted near the center comes out clean, 40 to 45 minutes. Cool 5 minutes before serving.
